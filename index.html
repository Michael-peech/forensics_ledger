System Protocol Guide

Standard Operating Procedures for the CYB-305 Immutable Ledger.

1. Identity & Access Control (RBAC)

Your cryptographic alias is deterministically generated from your wallet address. Access to evidence is strictly governed by the smart contract based on your assigned clearance:

  • Role 1 (Investigator): Authorized to seize and log initial evidence.
  • Role 2 (Clerk): Authorized to receive and vault physical assets.
  • Role 3 (Lab Tech): Authorized to check out assets and encode forensic matrices.
  • Role 4 (Liaison): Authorized to extract assets for legal/court review.

2. Module A: Initial Intake

Used strictly when discovering new evidence in the field.

Case Designation: Use standard operational syntax (e.g., CYB-305_Alpha).
Unique Item ID: Physical tracking barcode or identifier.
File Hash: Uploading a file generates a decentralized Walrus Blob ID. The blockchain only stores this hash, ensuring zero-knowledge privacy while guaranteeing the file is never tampered with.

3. Module B: Custody Transfer

To transfer an asset, you must provide the exact Case Name/Item ID and paste the associated Walrus ID to verify integrity.

Selecting a Transfer Action will actively query the blockchain and filter the recipient list. The smart contract will mathematically reject any transfer to an operative who lacks the required clearance for that action. Paste a raw 0x... address to verify a recipient's clearance in real-time.

4. Forensics Terminal (Lab Techs Only)

Assets checked out for "Forensic Imaging" or "Data Analysis" will appear in the recipient's Evidence Locker. Clicking the asset opens the Forensics Terminal.

Only the operative with current active custody can write to the Analysis and Evaluation matrices. Once encoded, the reports are permanently etched into the EVM state.
